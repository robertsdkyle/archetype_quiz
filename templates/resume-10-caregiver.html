· I · Purpose

Compassionate care and clinical excellence are not opposing forces.

I came to nursing because I wanted to do work that mattered the moment it left my hands. Twelve years later, that's still the test I apply to every shift. Did this matter to the person in front of me, today?

My practice has moved from the ICU through oncology and into clinical specialty work, but the orientation hasn't changed: meet patients where they are, listen longer than is comfortable, and advocate hardest for the ones least able to advocate for themselves.

Outside the hospital, I mentor early-career nurses, coordinate community health outreach, and hold a quiet conviction that the profession is at its best when it's at its most ordinary: present, careful, kind.
