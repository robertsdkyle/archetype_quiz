The Brief

Eight years of getting things done — without the jargon, without the drama.

A plain-language look at how I work, who I've worked with, and what's kept the lights on.

I've managed operations for companies ranging from 12-person startups to 400-person regional offices, and the work is always the same: find what's broken, fix it with your team, and make sure it stays fixed.

I'm not the loudest person in the room, but I'm usually the one people turn to when something actually needs to get done. That suits me. I'd rather ship a quiet, working system than a loud, broken one.

Good processes make good teams. Good teams make everything possible. Most of my job is making sure the second sentence stays true — by handling the first.

Outside of work I coach my kid's little league team, fix things around the house on weekends, and keep a slightly-too-detailed spreadsheet of every road trip I've taken since 2014. That last part probably tells you most of what you need to know.
