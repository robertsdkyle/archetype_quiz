Field Notes

I don't study environments from a distance. I work from inside them.

From the Sonoran Desert to the Alaskan coast, the work has always pulled me toward the edge of accessible data. To places where conventional methods fail and you have to build the protocol yourself, often in the snow, often at 3 a.m.

My research lives at the meeting point of ecology, technology, and lived terrain. I lead small teams through long field seasons, write the proposals that fund the next one, and translate what we find into systems that change how land is managed.

I'm based in Denver but spend roughly four months a year in the field. If you're working on something at the edge, let's talk.
