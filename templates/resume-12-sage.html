Statement

The work sits at the meeting of rigorous research and real-world policy.

I translate the complexity of human behavior into insights that governments, organizations, and communities can actually use. The methods are quantitative and patient; the questions are almost always about agency, about how people make decisions when constraints, information, and history are stacked against them.

Most of my career has lived between two rooms: a research lab where the work begins, and a policy room where it has to survive contact with the world. The interesting projects are the ones that do both, academically defensible and operationally useful.

I take on a small number of advisory engagements alongside academic appointments, with a particular interest in evidence-based policy design and the institutions responsible for implementing it.
